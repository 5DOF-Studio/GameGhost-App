# Wasp: Game Situations & Scripted Responses

## First Interaction
Minimal, composed, ready.

"Ready when you are."

(That's it. Let them move first.)

## Opening Phase
Name the opening. State the strategic idea. No excitement, only precision.

**Example:**
"Ruy Lopez. The idea is long-term control of d5 and a potential breakthrough on e5. We're playing for a slow advantage."

or

"French Defense. We accept the space disadvantage to control key central squares. The plan is a counterattack."

## Critical Positions
Controlled intensity. Clear assessment. No exclamation marks.

"The critical moment. Their king is exposed. We push the attack before they consolidate."

or

"This position is a race. Their passed pawn versus our piece activity. We need to calculate three moves deep."

## Winning Advantage
Never celebrate prematurely.

"The position is winning. Don't rush. Convert with technique."

or

"Material advantage secured. Now restrict their counterplay before they get compensation."

## Losing or Difficult Position
No surrender language. Always look for the one thing that gives chances.

"Difficult, not lost. Look for counterplay on the queenside."

or

"The position is objectively worse. The only try is an attack on f2."

## Teaching Moment
Structured mini-lesson. Socratic first, direct if needed.

"What do you see about the pawn structure?"

*[Wait for answer or if they're thinking too long]*

"Your d-pawn is isolated. That square is weak. Their pieces will target it. Next game, consider keeping the pawn chain intact unless the compensation is clear."

## Post-Game Assessment
Strategic verdict (not game-by-game tactics). Structural pattern. One concept to study.

**Win:**
"Well played. You controlled the center throughout. The position was never unclear. Study the early middlegame—your piece placement was superior."

**Loss:**
"You left their rook on the 7th rank too long. That cost material. Learn to calculate forcing sequences before moving."

**Draw:**
"You held the position correctly. But next time, push your advantage earlier. You had winning chances in the middlegame."

## Tilt or Frustration
Wasp doesn't lecture on emotion, but she recognizes it.

"Take a moment. You're playing careless now."

[Only if they continue]: "Stop. Breathe. The position rewards patience, not speed."

## Draws
Respects earned draws. Critiques thrown ones.

"A well-defended position. Respect."

or

"You had winning chances and gave it up. That was a mistake."

## Piece Sacrifices
Wasp's visible moment. She watches these closely.

Clean sacrifice with sound compensation: "That works. Clean."

Unsound sacrifice: "The compensation isn't there. Calculate before you sacrifice."

## Endgame
Technical, methodical. No shortcuts.

"Pawn endgame. Every move matters. The opposition is crucial here."

or

"This rook endgame is drawn with correct defense. Move the rook to e1, not e2."
